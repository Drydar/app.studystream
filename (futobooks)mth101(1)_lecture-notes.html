Real Numbers

Real numbers are used in everyday life to describe quantities such as age, population, etc. Real numbers are represented with numbers such as;

A real number is RATIONAL when it can be written as the ratio p/q of two integers, where q ≠ 0.
For instance, the numbers;
1/3 = 0.333…. ≈ 0.3, 1/8 = 0.125 and 125/111 = 1.126126…. are rational.
A real number that cannot be written as the ratio of two integers is called IRRATIONAL
Irrational numbers have infinite non-repeating decimal representation.

Field axioms of the real numbers

We now state the basic properties of real numbers
Let a, b and c be real numbers and let the two usual operations (addition and multiplication) in R be denoted by (+) and (–) respectively, then the following properties are satisfied:
Closure: a + b and a . b are also real numbers
Commutative law: a + b = b + a and a.b = b.a
Associative law: (a + b) + c = a + (b + c)
Distributive law: a.(b + c) = (a.b) + (a.c)
Additive identity: 0 is the additive identity since 0 + a = a
Multiplicative identity: a is the multiplicative identity since 1.a = a
Additive inverse: The additive inverse of a is –a. since a + (-a) = a – a = 0
Multiplicative inverse: The additive inverse of a is a-1 since a.a-1 = 1, provided a is different from 0

Graph of Real Line:

Real numbers are separated graphically by a number line (real line). The point 0 on the real is the origin. Numbers to the right of 0 are positive and numbers to the left of 0 are negative.
The term NON-NEGATIVE describes a number that is either positive or zero.

Ordering of real numbers:

One very important property of real numbers is that they are ordered.
Definition: If a and b are real numbers, then a is less than b when b – a is positive.
The order is denoted by the inequality a < b.
This relationship can also be described by saying that b is greater than a and writing b > a.
The inequality a ≤ b means that a is less than or equal to b and the inequality b ≥ a means that b is greater than or equal to a.
The symbols <, >, ≤ and ≥ are inequality symbols.
